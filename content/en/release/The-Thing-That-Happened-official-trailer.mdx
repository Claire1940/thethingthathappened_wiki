## 🏛️ Game Overview
The Thing That Happened is an open-world third-person action-adventure survival game set in a post-apocalyptic world. Players take on the role of Marcus Draven, a survivor navigating the dangerous Jericho city. The game focuses on resource management, crafting, combat, and exploration, with a deep lore and multiple endings.

## 📚 The Basics
### 🌿 Understanding the World
The world of The Thing That Happened is vast and dangerous. Players must navigate through abandoned buildings, dangerous streets, and hidden locations to gather resources and survive. The environment is filled with zombies, hostile gangs, and other threats that players must prepare for.

### 🛠️ Crafting and Resources
Crafting is a vital part of survival in The Thing That Happened. Players can craft weapons, armor, and other survival items using resources found throughout the world. Prioritizing the collection of essential materials is crucial for long-term survival.

### 🛡️ Security and Base Building
Setting up a secure base is essential for long-term survival. Players can build defenses, store resources, and recover between missions. A strong base becomes the foundation for exploring deeper into the world and facing more challenging enemies.

## 🎯 Advanced Tips
### 🎯 Resource Prioritization
Prioritize collecting essential resources such as food, water, and ammunition. These resources are crucial for survival and should be managed carefully to ensure a steady supply.

### 🎯 Combat Strategy

### 🎯 Exploration and Secrets
Exploring the world of The Thing That Happened reveals hidden secrets, lore, and additional resources. Keep an eye out for hidden locations, secret areas, and collectible items that can enhance your survival chances.

### 🤔 What happens if I regret my class choice?
Once you select a class in The Thing That Happened, the decision is permanent. However, you can create a new character if you want to explore a different class.

### 🤔 Can I play as a Mage but use a sword?
Yes, but it won't be as effective as choosing a class that complements that playstyle, like Knight or Rogue.
